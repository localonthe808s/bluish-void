Cumulus humilis small fair-weather puffs

Low cover Cu. 3-4 bumps each. Heights 7-12.

Cumulus mediocris moderate puffs

Bigger than humilis. 4-5 bumps. Sharper edges, more cauliflower.

Cumulus congestus towering vertical

Tall, deep. Wind shear tilt, darker base, smooth cap hint at top.

Cumulonimbus storm cloud

Tower + anvil + overshooting top + striations + virga + mammatus.

Stratocumulus 4 distinct sub-types

Coverage dispatches to: perlucidus (broken patches with sky breaks) → stratiformis (classic continuous rolls + per-roll sun lighting) → castellanus (turreted tops, instability sign) → opacus (thick uniform layer).

Altocumulus stratiformis honeycomb puffs (mackerel sky)

Mid-altitude lens-shaped puffs in a hex grid with atmospheric perspective (distant cells smaller, hazier).

Altostratus mid-altitude veil

Featureless gray-purple sheet. Sun shows through faintly when translucidus (cover < 80%).

Stratus low overcast — v3 dispatch

cover<50% → H (fractus lenses on faint base veil); 60–80% → G (layered depth, paler back + bolder front); else → E (continuous wavy mass + horizontal density bands). Internal variation NEVER as dots.

Nimbostratus heavy precipitating layer

Dense opaque overcast that lowers as coverage increases. Stratus-fractus rags hang below the base, virga curtains descend, sun-side rim glow catches the bottom edge. Precip dispatches: 80%→light rain, 90%→moderate, 95%→heavy shafts, 100%→snow.

Cirrocumulus high small puffy cells

High-altitude small puffs in patches. Each cell ~5 wide. Pink-tinted undersides.

Cirrostratus high thin veil + 22° halo

Pink-tinted sheet with embedded horizontal fibers, color-graded by altitude. Solar halo arc visible.

Cirrus fibratus parallel fiber filaments (default)

v3 — long horizontal lens shapes + many parallel fiber strokes. Fibrous brushstrokes, NOT puffy ellipses.

Cirrus uncinus hook commas

v3 — small dense head + long curving tail that drops then trails. Hook trails away from sun. 4-layer rendering: halo, fibrous mid, bright core, secondary fibers.

Cirrus spissatus dense / thick

Same lens structure as fibratus but thicker, denser body. Often residue from dissipating Cb anvils.

Cirrus intortus tangled / disordered

Lens body + extra crisscrossing fibers (high-altitude turbulence signature).
